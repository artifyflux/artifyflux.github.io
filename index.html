A Quick Guide to Prompting



1. Sentences: The prompt should be structured in clear and concise sentences. While multiple sentences can be used to elaborate on the prompt, it is advisable to avoid overly complicated language or technical jargon that the AI might misinterpret.


2. Commas: Using commas within sentences is an excellent practice for separating details in a prompt. This approach prevents unnecessary complexity while allowing the model to process the information more effectively. Incorporating commas strategically can enhance the prompt's clarity and precision.


3. Specificity: Ensure that the prompt is as specific as possible, avoiding vague or ambiguous phrases. Clearly outline the desired features, such as colors, textures, and styles, to guide the model in generating the most accurate output.


4. Object and Subject Focus: Identify the primary object or subject of the prompt and prioritize its description. Supporting details should complement the main focus without overshadowing or diluting the central theme.


5. Avoid Overloading Details: While it is essential to provide sufficient details, overloading the prompt with excessive information can confuse the model. Maintain a balance between specificity and simplicity to achieve optimal results.


6. Logical Flow: Organize the prompt in a logical sequence, beginning with the primary subject and gradually introducing additional details. This structured approach helps the model interpret and integrate the information cohesively.


7. Use of Adjectives: Incorporate adjectives judiciously to describe attributes like texture, size, shape, or mood. However, avoid using overly complex or abstract adjectives that might lead to unintended interpretations.


8. Avoid Contradictions: Ensure that the details provided in the prompt are consistent and do not contradict each other. Conflicting instructions can result in inaccurate or unsatisfactory outputs.


9. Contextual Clarity: If the prompt involves a specific setting or environment, provide contextual information to guide the model. This helps in creating a coherent background that aligns with the central object or subject.


10. Iterative Refinement: It is recommended to refine and test the prompt iteratively. Experiment with slight variations in wording and structure to identify the most effective phrasing for achieving the desired output.

Usage



1. Prompt: The prompt must be meticulously detailed and articulated with utmost clarity, focusing on the fundamental concepts and core elements of your desired depiction. Ensuring precision in your prompt will significantly enhance the quality of the generated output.


2. Entering: You should carefully enter your prompt into the designated input bar provided within the form interface. After ensuring the accuracy of the prompt, proceed to click on the "Generate" button to initiate the image generation process.


3. Time: The generation process typically requires approximately 180 to 240 seconds to complete. During this period, it is strongly advised to remain patient and stay on the webpage to allow the process to complete without interruptions.


4. Closing: Under no circumstances should you close the webpage or refresh it while the generation is in progress. Such actions may result in the loss of your generated image. It is essential to remain on the page and wait until the process is fully completed.


5. Canvas: Once the image generation is successfully completed, the canvas will display your generated image. For desktop clients, advanced functionalities such as zooming and panning are available, allowing you to explore your image in greater detail.


6. Download: The "Download" button will become active only after the image has been successfully generated. Upon clicking this button, the image will be promptly saved to your device, ensuring a seamless download experience.
